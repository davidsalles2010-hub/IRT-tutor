How it works

1

Answer ~15 questions

Every answer changes what comes next. Get one right and the next is a little harder; miss one and it eases off — zeroing in on the edge of their ability.

2

We measure, not grade

Behind the scenes, an Item Response Theory model — the same family of statistics behind exams like the GRE — estimates true ability, not just percent correct.

3

Get a skill map

Finish with a clear report: an overall level with a confidence range, a topic-by-topic map, and the specific areas where practice will pay off most.

Why adaptive beats a fixed quiz

A fixed quiz

Gives everyone the same 20 questions. Strong students coast through easy ones that reveal nothing; struggling students face walls of problems they can't attempt. The score mostly reflects which questions happened to be on the sheet.

A MathLens diagnostic

Spends every question where it's most informative — right at the frontier of what the student knows. Fifteen well-chosen questions pin down ability more precisely than a fixed quiz twice as long, and the misses show exactly what to practice.
